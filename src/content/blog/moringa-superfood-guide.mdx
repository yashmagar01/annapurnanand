## What Is Moringa?

Moringa oleifera — commonly called the "Miracle Tree" or "Drumstick Tree" — is one of the most nutrient-dense plants on Earth. Native to the Himalayan foothills and now thriving along fertile riverbelt soils like those of the **Godavari region**, Moringa has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for over 4,000 years.

As a practitioner with both a BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine) and an MPH-N (Master of Public Health in Nutrition), I've spent the last decade studying plants that bridge traditional wisdom and modern nutritional science. Moringa tops that list every single time.

## How to Incorporate Moringa into Your Daily Routine

The best supplement is the one you actually take consistently. Here are three foolproof methods:

### 1. The Morning Boost (1 minute)
Mix 1 teaspoon (5g) in a glass of lukewarm water with a squeeze of lemon. The vitamin C in lemon enhances iron absorption by up to 67%.

### 2. The Family Smoothie (5 minutes)
Add 1 teaspoon to your morning smoothie. The earthy flavour blends beautifully with banana, mango, or any tropical fruit.

### 3. The Cook's Method (0 minutes extra)
Add Moringa powder to dals, soups, or chutneys during the last 2 minutes of cooking. Heat-sensitive nutrients are preserved when added at the end.

## Safety and Dosage

Moringa is classified as **GRAS (Generally Recognized as Safe)** by food safety authorities worldwide. Our products are FSSAI licensed and tested for heavy metals, microbial contamination, and pesticide residues at every batch.

**Recommended daily dose:** 1–2 teaspoons of powder, or 2 capsules.

**Who should consult a doctor first:**
- Pregnant women (Moringa root bark can cause uterine contractions — leaf powder is safe)
- Those on blood-thinning medications (Moringa has mild anticoagulant properties)
- Those with thyroid conditions (start with smaller doses)
